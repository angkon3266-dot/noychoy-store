# Voice & tone

## Voice (always)
Warm, personal, confident — like a stylish friend who knows jewelry, not a
salesperson. Short sentences. Concrete sensory detail ("catches light with
every movement") over abstract praise ("stunning quality").

## Register by channel
- **Product pages:** romantic but precise — one emotional line, then facts
  (materials, dimensions, care). English primary; sprinkle Bangla occasion
  words naturally (Eid, gaye holud, boishakh) where they fit.
- **Push notifications:** ≤ 8-word titles, one emoji max, benefit first.
  ("Your offer expires tonight 🎁", not "NOTICE: promotional offer").
- **SMS:** plain text, no emoji dependence (delivery varies), include the
  store name and one clear action. Costs money — every word earns its place.
- **Facebook ads:** hook in the first line, social proof early ("Loved by
  customers"), COD trust line near the CTA.
- **Support replies:** warm + apologetic when we erred, never defensive;
  always end with the concrete next step and timing.

## Words we use
shine, glow, treasured, handpicked, everyday luxury, brilliance, timeless,
gift-ready, cash on delivery, yours.

## Words we avoid
cheap, discount blast, guaranteed (unless literally guaranteed), solid gold /
real diamond (for plated/CZ items — see [materials](../materials/cubic-zirconia.md)),
"limited stock!!!" urgency theatrics, ALL CAPS.

## Formatting rules
- Prices always with ৳ and thousand separators: ৳1,450.
- Title Case for product names; sentence case for everything else.
- One CTA per message. The CTA states the low-risk path: "Order now — pay on
  delivery."

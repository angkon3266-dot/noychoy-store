# Cubic zirconia (CZ)

## What it is
A lab-created crystal cut and polished like a diamond. Optically it delivers
diamond-level sparkle and clear white brilliance; chemically it is zirconium
dioxide, not carbon diamond. In BD it's often called "American diamond (AD)".

## Why we use it
Diamond-look brilliance at a fraction of the price — the heart of accessible
everyday luxury. Consistent quality (no mined-stone flaws), ethical, and
replaceable if ever lost.

## How it compares
- **vs diamond:** to the eye, comparable fire and sparkle; CZ is slightly
  less hard (8–8.5 vs 10 Mohs), so it can dull after years of rough daily
  wear where a diamond wouldn't. Price difference: ~100×.
- **vs crystal/glass:** CZ is noticeably brighter, better cut, more durable.

## Care instructions
Wipe with a soft dry cloth after wear; avoid perfume/hairspray directly on
stones; store pieces separately so stones don't scratch each other; a soapy
lukewarm rinse + soft brush restores sparkle (dry fully).

## Copywriting notes
- Sell it as: "diamond-look brilliance", "sparkling cubic zirconia",
  "AD stone" (local familiarity).
- **Never** call it "diamond" or "real diamond". "Diamond-cut sparkle" is
  acceptable; "diamond" as a noun for the stone is not.

## FAQ
**Is it a real diamond?** No — it's cubic zirconia, a lab-made stone with
diamond-like sparkle. That honesty is why the price is this good.
**Will it lose its shine?** With basic care it stays brilliant for years;
avoid chemicals and store it dry.

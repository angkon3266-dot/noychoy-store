# Rhodium plating

## What it is
A thin layer of rhodium — a precious platinum-group metal — electroplated
over the base metal. It's what gives fine white-toned jewelry its bright,
mirror-like, slightly cool shine. Real white gold jewelry is rhodium plated
too.

## Why we use it
- The brightest white finish available; makes CZ stones look their best.
- **Tarnish and scratch resistant** — the practical reason plated pieces
  survive daily wear in BD humidity.
- Hypoallergenic barrier between skin and base metal — kinder to sensitive
  skin than raw brass/alloy.

## Care instructions
Plating is durable but finite: keep away from perfume, sweat-soaked storage,
and chlorine; wipe after wear; store in a dry pouch. With care, years of
shine; heavy abrasion (gym, cleaning work) wears any plating faster.

## Copywriting notes
Sell: "rhodium plated for lasting shine", "the same finish used on white
gold". Never claim the piece is solid platinum/white gold.

## FAQ
**Will my skin react?** Rhodium is hypoallergenic; reactions are rare.
**Does it fade?** Very slowly with normal wear — avoid chemicals and it
keeps its mirror finish for years.
